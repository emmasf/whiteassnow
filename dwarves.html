The Dwarves are outsiders.

Disney was the first to sweeten these outsiders, giving the Dwarves individual names and personalities. Snow White usually spends her exile more as an indentured servant than a cherished guest, her beauty having no power over these men. Other versions of the story cast seven robbers, gypsies, eunuchs or other “strange people” in place of the Dwarves, and they often treat Snow White as an outsider herself.

They are uncivilized outcasts operating outside society's rules, having little regard for Snow White's beauty or status.
