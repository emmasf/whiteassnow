We all commit original sin.

Botanists and many religious scholars suggest that the forbidden fruit was a pomegranate, not an apple. In keeping with this theory, versions of the tale from the German Richilda to the Moroccan The Woman and the Sun describe the Queen enchanting the young girl with a pomegranate.

Disney reduced Snow White's temptation to just the apple, but in the Grimm tale and most other versions there are a series of three encounters. In most cases, these objects are symbols of the girl's budding womanhood: a corset, a comb, or a necklace. And what is fruit but a plant's ripe sexual organ?

We can't help it. Snow White bites into the apple as Eve did before her. Temptation is too sweet, the desire to transform too enticing.
