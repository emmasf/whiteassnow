When you think of Snow White, you probably picture the film that started the Disney empire. The tale was first published by Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm in their 1812 book, Kinder-und Hausmaerchen, or Tales of Children and the Home.

The brothers were not writing for children; they were academic linguists, interested in preserving German language and culture and in strengthening the German identity. They set out to collect German oral traditions and capture them for the ages. Along with the old German words themselves, the brothers felt that a lot of traditional values lay in the stories they recorded.

They may have been right, but this story is not uniquely German. Versions of the story appear all over the globe: Scotland, Ireland, France, Spain, Portugal, Italy, Hungary, Romania, Albania, Greece, Morocco, Algeria, Libya, West Africa, Gabon, Angola and Mozambique, to name a few. Details vary, but the tale of a young beauty, a jealous older woman, exile, magical poisoning, and a long coma are universal.
