Snow White is a story about women passing through phases of life: a maiden becomes a mother, a child becomes a maiden, and a mother becomes a crone.

It may be tempting to trace the lack of men to the idea that bedtime tales are the domain of women and children. However, many versions of this tale and others feature a prominent father figure; it seems to be just the Grimm versions that are lacking in men. This recurring female focus may be due to the authors' childhood: their father died when they were very young, leaving the brothers without a prominent male figure.
