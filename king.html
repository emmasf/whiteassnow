The King

is absent.

You would think he might notice that his wife is psychotic and his daughter has gone missing.

Snow White is born with no father in sight. In the Grimm tale, the King appears only in a single reference to his remarriage. This is mostly a story about women passing through phases of life: a maiden becomes a mother, a child becomes a maiden, and a mother becomes a crone.

It may be tempting to trace the lack of men to the idea that bedtime tales are the domain of women and children, but the father is more involved in many versions of the tale. In the case of the Grimm tales, this recurring lack of men may be due to the authors' childhood: their father left when they were very young, leaving the brothers without a prominent male figure.
